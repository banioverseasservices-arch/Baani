Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about studying in Australia

Most Australian universities require a high school diploma (12th pass) with good grades. Specific requirements vary by university and course. We help you identify universities matching your profile.

Typically 3-4 months from initial counselling to receiving an offer. This includes university selection, application preparation, and submission. The timeline may vary based on individual circumstances.

International tuition fees range from AUD 15,000-45,000 per year depending on the course. Living expenses are approximately AUD 18,000-25,000 annually. We provide detailed cost breakdowns and scholarship guidance.

Most universities require English proficiency test scores (IELTS/PTE). However, some universities offer direct entry or pathway programs for Indian students. We guide you on whether you need to take the test.

After receiving an offer from a university, you receive a CoE (Confirmation of Enrolment). You then apply for a Student Visa (subclass 500) through the Department of Home Affairs. The process typically takes 4-8 weeks.

Yes! International students can work up to 20 hours per week during semester and full-time during holidays. This helps supplement living expenses and gain work experience in Australia.

Many Australian universities offer merit-based scholarships covering 25-100% of tuition fees. We help you identify and apply for scholarships matching your academic profile and financial needs.

After graduation, you can apply for a Post-Study Work visa, allowing you to work in Australia for 1-4 years depending on your qualification. This is an excellent pathway to permanent residency or career development.
